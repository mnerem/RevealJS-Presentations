Scientific Notation

Mass of an object that contains three hundred-thousand-billion-billion atoms of carbon given that one carbon atom has a mass of twenty billion-billion-billionths of a kilogram?

$$ \begin{align*} M&=N_\text{atoms}\times m_\text{C} \\ &=300,000,000,000,000,000,000,000 \times \\ & \qquad 0.000,000,000,000,000,000,000,000,02\,\text{kg} \\ &=\big(3E23\big)\big(2E-26\big)\text{kg}\\ &=\big(3\times 2\big)\big(10^{23}\times10^{-26}\big)\,\text{kg}\\ &=6\times10^{23-26}\,\text{kg} \\ &=6E-3\,\text{kg} \\ &=6\,\text{grams} \end{align*} $$ Scientific notation gives us a way to easily communicate the “size” of a number.

\begin{align} &=\frac{8}{4}10^{1024-1020}\times\frac{1}{2}10^{-3} \\ &=\frac{8}{4\times2}10^4\times10^{-3}\\ &=\frac{8}{8}10^{4-3}\\ &=1\times10^1\\ &=10 \end{align}

Slope of a Curve

Where and how you measure the slope are very important.
  • Draw a tangent line
    (just touches the curve at a point)
  • Approximate the slope using a "rise-run" to get the average rate of change $$\text{Slope} \approx \frac{\Delta y}{\Delta x}$$
  • Derivative tells us the instantaneous rate of change $$\text{Slope} = \frac{d y}{d x}$$

Quadratics: $ax^2+bx+c=0$


$$\text{Quadratic Formula: } x=\frac{-b\pm\sqrt{b^2-4ac}}{2a}$$ Physics tells us if we use the $+$ or $-$ solution
(or complex numbers).

PQ: Kinematics Interception

Bruce is jogging at pace of 2.5 m/s and passes Tony who is sitting on a park bench. When Bruce is 3 m ahead, Tony gets up and tries to catch up to Bruce. Tony runs with a constant acceleration of 1 m/s$^2$. At what time will Tony catch up to Bruce? The following are the standard kinematic equations. \begin{gather} x_f=x_i+v_0t+\tfrac{1}{2}at^2 \\ v_f=v_i+at \\ v_f^2=v_i^2+2a\Delta x \end{gather}
