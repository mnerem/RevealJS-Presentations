28: Sources of Magnetism

Biot-Savart Law

\begin{equation} d\vec{B}=\frac{\mu_0}{4\pi}Id\vec{\ell}\times\frac{\hat{r}}{r^2} \end{equation}

$\mu_0$= magnetic permeability = $4\pi\times10^{-7}$Tm/A
AKA magnetic constant
AKA vacuum permeability

B-Field of a Point Charge

\begin{equation} \vec{B}= \frac{\mu_0}{4\pi}I\vec{v}\times\frac{\hat{r}}{r^2} \end{equation}

B-Field of a Finite length Current

Consider a wire of current $I$ and length $2a$.
\begin{gather} d\vec{B}= \frac{\mu_0}{4\pi}Id\vec{\ell}\times\frac{\hat{r}}{r^2} \\ d\vec{\ell}=dy\,\hat{y} \\ \vec{r}=x\,\hat{x}-y\,\hat{y} \\ \vdots \end{gather}
\begin{equation} |\vec{B}|=\frac{\mu_oI}{4\pi}\frac{2a}{x\sqrt{x^2+a^2}} \end{equation}
Can model wire to be $\infty$ long when $x\ll a$.

B-Field of a Wire of Current

\begin{gather} |\vec{B}_\text{wire}|= \frac{\mu_0 I}{2\pi r} \end{gather}
